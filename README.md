# Hip Dysplasia Fundamentals

An open-source educational resource for medical students and junior residents covering hip dysplasia fundamentals.

## Purpose

This repository aims to provide a structured, practical guide for understanding the basics of hip dysplasia, focusing on clinical signs, imaging interpretation, and initial management strategies. It serves as a collaborative educational resource that can be freely shared and improved by the medical community.

## Target Audience

- Medical students
- Junior residents in orthopedics, radiology, and pediatrics
- Any healthcare professional seeking foundational knowledge on hip dysplasia

## Planned Content Sections

1. Introduction to Hip Dysplasia
2. Clinical Signs and Symptoms
3. Imaging Interpretation
   - Radiographic measurements (LCE angle, acetabular index, Shenton's line)
   - Advanced imaging (MRI, CT)
4. Initial Management Strategies
5. Surgical and Non-surgical Treatment Options
6. Case Studies and Clinical Pearls
7. References and Further Reading
